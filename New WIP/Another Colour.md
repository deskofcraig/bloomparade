- C = ♭7th of D minor ✓

### Key slide moves on C6

#### Dm11 → Gm11 (the main loop move)

From **open** (Dm11) to **fret 1 partial on strings 2 and 6** (Gm11) — a single-fret movement on just two strings while four strings stay open and ringing. At 123 BPM this is a quick, light touch. The continuity of the open strings creates a natural legato blend between chords.

#### Gm11 → C11

From **fret 1 partial** (strings 2, 6) back to **open strings 1–3, 5, 6** plus fret 1 on string 4 only. The C11 chord is essentially open except for string 4. This is a minimal motion transition.

#### The Picardy moment (Dm11 → Dmaj)

From **fully open** to a **full bar at fret 2** — the most dramatic single movement in the track. Use a slow volume swell: fade out the open chord, slide the bar to fret 2 with no volume, then swell back in on the Dmaj. The silence before the F# lands makes it hit harder.

### Melodic phrases on C6 for D minor

- **D minor pentatonic runs:** Str 3 open (G) → fret 2 (A) → fret 5 (C) → fret 7 (D). Or descending: fret 7→5→2→open on string 3.
- **Bb reach:** String 4 fret 1 (Bb) — the one diatonic note not on an open string. Slide to it from open A (string 4) for a characteristic ♭6th sigh, particularly during Gm11 sections.
- **Aeolian descending run:** String 2: fret 8 (C) → fret 7 (B... careful: B is not in D minor) — stay at fret 8 → fret 5 (A) → fret 3 (G) → fret 1 (F) → open (E). The E is the 9th of Dm; let it ring.
- **Picardy prep phrase:** During the Fmaj11 section before the outro, play melodic line resolving to F# (string 2 fret 2 or string 6 fret 2) — telegraphing the major resolution to come.
- **Open string drone throughout Gm/Dm alternation (2:16–3:28):** Simply rest the bar lightly at fret 2 and let strings 1, 3, 4, 5 ring open — the sound shifts naturally between Dm and G colour depending on which partial bar strings you engage. This meditative section suits minimal movement.

### Avoid on C6 for this track

- **Fret 4 on any string** — produces G# / Ab, which is foreign to D Aeolian. Avoid entirely.
- **Fret 9 on strings 2 or 6** — gives C#, outside the key.
- **B natural (string 2 fret 7, string 6 fret 7)** — not in D Aeolian; brings a Dorian flavour that doesn't fit most of the track (though could be used deliberately during Gm sections for brief colour).
- **F# (fret 2) before the outro** — F# is the Picardy note. Using it earlier weakens the resolution impact. Reserve it exclusively for the Dmaj outro moment.
