## C6 Lap Steel — CEGACE

### How C6 sits in D major

Open C6 strings: **C · E · G · A · C · E**

In D major (D E F# G A B C#), the open strings C, E, G, A map as follows:
- E = 9th of D major / 2nd degree ✓
- G = 4th/11th of D major ✓
- A = 5th of D major ✓
- C = ♭7th — the **one non-diatonic open string**

C natural is not in D major (D major has C#). The open C strings introduce a ♭7th colour, which gives the C6 a slightly Mixolydian quality over D major chords. This is not a problem — it adds a warm, slightly earthy tint — but it means that **string 1 and string 5 (both C) should be used with awareness**: they will sound slightly outside a pure D major voicing unless the bar is placed at fret 1 to raise them to C#.

The exception is the **A11 and Em11 chords**, where C natural is entirely diatonic and the open strings serve perfectly.

### Key insight: Dmaj11 vs A11 / Em11 on C6

The open C6 chord (C E G A C E) sounds like **Am11** or **C6** — notes entirely inside D major except for the C natural. Over the Dmaj11 chord, these open strings create a lush extended colour (C = ♭7, E = 9, G = 11, A = 5). Over A11 and Em11 they are completely diatonic. The track's harmonic world is unusually forgiving for C6.
