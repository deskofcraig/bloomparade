This is the most harmonically ambiguous track in this series. The picture that emerges from the chord content is:

**The track lives in the G major / E natural minor scale (G A B C D E F#), with E minor as the deepest emotional home and D as the most frequently visited chord.**

Three converging pieces of evidence support E minor as the tonal centre:

The D11 chord (D F# A C E G — dominant 11th with a ♭7) appears repeatedly as a transitional chord. This chord uses C natural as the flat seventh, giving these passages a **D Mixolydian flavour** — D acting momentarily as a local tonic with a characteristic ♭7. This is a normal feature of Aeolian harmony: the ♭VII chord (D) tends to take on Mixolydian colouring when it functions locally.

### The Dm11 intrusion

At 0:24, 1:12, and 2:32, a **Dm11** chord (D F A C E G) appears briefly — D minor rather than D major. D minor is not native to E Aeolian (which has D major). These passages represent a brief **parallel minor colour shift**, importing the F natural (♭3rd of D) as a chromatic shadow note. They are transitional and short-lived — a momentary darkening before the track returns to the main harmonic world.

### The B11 moment

At 2:04, a **B11** chord (B D# F# A C# E) appears once. B major is not in E Aeolian (E Aeolian has Bm). This borrowed chord functions as a **secondary dominant** — V/VIm, or V of Em — creating the only moment of genuine dominant tension in the track before resolving to Em11 at 2:08. The D# (leading tone to E) makes this the closest the track comes to a classical cadence.

## Synthesiser Recommendations

### Safe scale — use throughout
**E natural minor: E F# G A B C D**

This works across all chords in the track. The one addition to keep available: **C#** — it appears in Dmaj11, Bm11, and B11, and can be used as an expressive leading tone during those chords. Over D11 and Cmaj11 use C natural; over Dmaj11 and Bm11 use C#. The alternation between C and C# mirrors the D11/Dmaj11 ambiguity in the track itself.

### Pad voicings

- **Em11 (tonic, structural anchor):** E G B D F# A — a full minor 11th pad. Use the warmest, most enveloping pad sound in the palette here; this is the home position that frames the entire piece. Slow attack, very long release — the Em11 should feel like arriving somewhere.
- **Dmaj11 (primary movement):** D F# A C# E G — a bright major 11th. A slightly elevated, more energetic pad texture than Em11. The C# distinguishes this from D11 and gives it a cleaner, more resolved major quality.
- **D11 (Mixolydian float):** D F# A C E G — almost identical to Dmaj11 but with C natural replacing C#. A slightly darker, warmer pad; the C natural is the defining sound of the Mixolydian ♭VII character. Subtly morph pad brightness between D11 and Dmaj11 versions — this ambiguity is part of the track's harmonic personality.
- **Cmaj11 (warmth):** C E G B D F — a major 11th pad on C. The B (maj7th) and F (11th — actually a tritone against C, but used for colour) give this chord its particularly rich, complex quality. A lush, slightly string-like pad works well here.
- **Dm11 (shadow passages):** D F A C E G — the F natural and A together define the minor quality against D. Use a noticeably darker pad texture for these brief moments; the shift from Dmaj11 to Dm11 should be perceptible as a cloud crossing the sun.
- **B11 (bridge tension, 2:04):** B D# F# A C# E — the D# is the key tension note. A brief, slightly brighter or more forward pad texture here telegraphs the secondary dominant tension before the Em11 resolution. This is the only moment in the track where forward harmonic tension is genuinely present.

### Lead / melodic palette

- **E minor pentatonic: E G A B D** — the safest and most characteristic melodic palette. Works across every chord in the track, including the Dm11 and D11 sections.
- **Full E Aeolian: E F# G A B C D** — the complete scale. F# adds colour over Em11; C and D give the Mixolydian/Aeolian character over the D chords.
- **C# use:** Over Dmaj11, Bm11, and B11 specifically — the major 7th of D and the 9th of B. Use as an approach note resolving to D (over Dmaj11) or to B (over Bm11). Do not use C# over D11 or Cmaj11 — it creates mild clashes.
- **F natural:** Appears in Dm11 and Cmaj11 (as the 11th of C). Over Cmaj11, F can be used as a passing tone — it is the Lydian 4th of C and creates a beautiful colour before resolving back to E. Over Dm11, F is the minor 3rd of D minor — a darker, blues-adjacent sound.
- **D# use:** Only during B11 at 2:04. The D# as a leading tone resolving upward to E is the primary melodic gesture available at that moment. One or two notes maximum before the Em11 arrives and the scale returns to D natural.
